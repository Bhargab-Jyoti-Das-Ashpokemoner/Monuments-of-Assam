Khaspur Ruins

Overview

A beautiful village, Khaspur is located only 20 km from Silchar, in Cachar district of Assam. It houses the ruins of the Dimasa Kingdom, which are a magnificent sight to see. There is a king’s temple, a Lion Gate, and a Sun Gate and these are centuries old. The king’s palace is almost non-existence but the main entrance gates still stands tall. These gates are in elephant pattern. Originally a part of the Tripura Kingdom, Khaspur was ruled by a tributary ruler. It became independent after the fall of Koch power. As the last of the Koch ruler died, the control of Khaspur went into the hands of Kachari rulers and they shifted their capital to Khaspur. Now this place lie in ruins which was once a very powerful kingdom of its time.

How to get there?

Khaspur has no railway station nearby. Silchar is the connecting link and this is where we need to travel to. Silchar railways station, 20 km away is the nearest one and there are regular trains to major cities of India from here. Many rental cabs are available from this station. Silchar is a well connected place by air, rail and road with other parts of Assam and the country.

Best Time to visit

Summer is hot and humid with unexpected showers now and then. It rains heavily during monsoons as well. Winter is dry and cool and is the best time to visit this place.
