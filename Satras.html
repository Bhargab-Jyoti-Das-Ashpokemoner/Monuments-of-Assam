Satras

Overview

If you are Assamese you may be familiar with the word naam naamghars and satras. But many people don't know the historical, cultural and religious importance of these Satras. Keep reading to know more about these amazing Satras.
Satras are institutional centers associated with the Ekasarana tradition of Vaishnavism, largely found in the Indian state of Assam and neighboring regions. These centers, in the minimum, maintain a prayer house, initiate lay people into the Ekasarana tradition and include them as disciples of the Satra from whom taxes and other religious duties are extracted. Sankaradeva is said to have established his first Satra at Bardowa, his birthplace, and then in different places of Assam. The Neo-Vaishnavite satra culture started in the 16th century. They grew rapidly in the 17th century and patronage extended to them by first the Koch kingdom and later the Ahom kingdom was crucial in the spread the Ekasarana religion. Another satra was established by a king named Samudrapal at a place known as Yogihati in the same period (1232 AD) as evident from a stone inscription found in Ambari. Many of the larger Satras house hundreds of celibate and non-celibate bhakats. They not only worship Vishnu and Krishna but also are wonderful artisans and make masks, musical instruments as well as hand-fans and door frames. The last quarter of the 16th century saw the satras divide into four different sects.

  1. Brahmasamhati

    Damodaradeva formed the Brahmasamhati. It represented the brahmanical practices and ritualism. It brought back the caste system to its original form but still adhered to the teachings of namadharma of Sankardev. Auniati satra, Dakhinpat Satra, Garamur satra and Kurawabahi satra are the most influential satras of this Samhati.

  2. Purusasamhati

    Caturbhuja Thakur, one of the grandson of Sankardev headed the Purusasamhati. This samhati practiced the original features of Sankardev teachings and therefore considered to be the main sect. The head priest was chosen either from a Kayastha or a Brahmin family. Bardowa satra is the most popular satra of this samhati.

  3. Nikasamhati

    The Nikasamhati was established by Mathuradasa and Padma Ata, both of whom were disciples of Madhavdeva. Kamalabari Satra, Barpeta Satra and Madhupur Satra are the three centers of this samhati.

  4. Kalasamhati

    The last of the samhati, the Kalasamhati concerned with the tribal and lowercaste societies, represented the offshoots of six sudra satra and six brahmana satra formed by Gopala Ata and his followers during the late 16th and early 17th centuries. The Mayamara satra and Dihing Satra, the two most important of the sudra satras.


After reading this you might actually feel an urge to visit some of those beautiful satras. However there are not one or two but many and many satras in Assam. Here are some of the most famous satras which are attractions for tourists.

  • Auniati Satra

    Founded by Niranjan Pathakdeva, the Satra is famous for "Paalnaam" and Apsara dances. Also remarkable is it's extensive assortment of ancient artefacts, Assamese old utensils, jewellery and handicrafts.

  • Dakhinpat Satra

    Founded by Banamalidev, an exponent of Raasleela, which is now observed as one of the main festivals of Assam. During Rasotsava several thousand devotees visit these holy Satras every year.

  • Garamur Satra

    Founded by Lakshmikantadeva. During the end of Autumn, traditional Raasleela (co-acting) is shown with great enthusiasm. Some ancient weapons called Bortop are preserved here.

  • Kamalabari Satra

    Founded by Bedulapadma Ata, it is a centre of art, culture, literature and classical studies. The finest boats on the island are made here. It's branch, Uttar Kamalabari Satra, has showcased the Satria Art in several states of India and abroad.

  • Bengena-ati Satra

    It is a storehouse of antiques of cultural importance and an advanced centre of performing art. Muraridev, the grand son of Sankardeva's step mother founded this Satra. The royal robes belonging to the Ahom king Swargadeo Gadadhar Singha, is preserved here. Also on display is the royal gold umbrella.

  • Shamaguri Satra

    The satra is famous worldwide for it's Mask-making tradition.

  • Barpeta Satra

    When Madhabdeva left the Satra, he gave the responsibility to his disciple Sri Mathuradasa Ata (1490-1596 AD), a Chandala by origin, to take care of the Satra and he became the first Satradhikar of Barpeta Satra. It was Burha Ata who systematized the administration of the Satra leading to development of the institution and the region of Barpeta. He introduced a democratic system which is effective till today. This is one of the most important satras. It is also famous worldwide for its grand celebration of Holi.

How to Get there?

To reach Barpeta, the rail route, via Katihar and Siliguri, is convenient. The nearest railway station is Barpeta Road. Nearest airport is at Guwahati. Barpeta is well connected by road transport from Guwahati (distance is 95 km).
Majuli is a famous island in the River Bramhaputra, flowing in the state of Assam. It is the biggest district island in the country and is quite close to Jorhat. It is not too difficult to commute to Majuli from Jorhat. We can reach Jorhat by flights from Guwahati as well as other parts of the country. We can also reach Jorhat by train and then by ferry and bus rides we can easily reach the satra there.
If we want to go to Bordowa, from Nagaon, we will find regular bus and cab services to Bordowa at specific times. The buses are of Assam State Transportation Corporation that reaches Bordowa through a well connected road network from Jorhat in about 4 hours. Thus we can see that the satras are well connected by different modes of transport and are easily reachable.

Best time to visit

Usually, any season and time is suitable for visiting a satra. But it is more fun if we visit during some special occasions. The best time to visit Barpeta Satra is during Holi. During Holi, pilgrims from different parts of the state, country and even the entire world come to see the grand occasion of Holi here which is one of the largest celebrations of the festival. It is most suitable to visit Majuli and many other satras during the occasion of Raas. During Paal naam, Titi of Sankardev and Madhavdev too, many people visit satras.
