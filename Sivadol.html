Sivadol, Sivasagar

Overview

Let us see another monument, temple in specific which is of great historic significance along with religious importance related to Shiva and that too in Sivasagar.
Siva dol is a group of structures comprising three Hindu temples of Sivadol, Vishnudol (meaning temple of the Lord Vishnu) and Devidol(means temple of the Goddess Devi Durga in the local Assamese language) shrines, and a museum.

  • Sivadol

    This famous Shiva Temple is located on the shores of the Sibsagar Lake and was constructed in 1734. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Shivadol was built by Bar Raja Ambika, queen of Ahom King Swargadeo Siba Singha. It is the tallest Shiva temple in India with a height of about 32 m and base perimeter of 59 m. Sibsagar, present day Sivasagar, was the capital of Ahom kingdom. The Ahoms had migrated from South China in 1228 and established their first capital at Charaideo 28 kilometres (17 mi) from present day Sivsagar in 1253. Initially they were Buddhists, though Hinduism came to prevail. It was the capital of the Ahom Kingdom till the British Raj was established in India. The tank was constructed between 1731 and 1738 and the temples were built in 1734 by Bar Raja Ambika, queen of Ahom king Swargadeo Siba Singha.

  • Vishnudol and Devidol

    Sivadol is surrounded by two subsidiary temples Vishnudol (spelt as Bishnudol) and Devidol which are architecturally similar to the larger Shiva temple.Vishnudol is dedicated to the God Vishnu. Devidol, also known as Joidol, is a Goddess Durga temple. While the Vishnu temple's tower has a honeycomb design with an ornamental criss-cross framework, the Durga temple's tower is designed, in its vertical elevation, with an undulating pattern. Besides these there is a temple of Ram and Hanuman.

  • Festivals in Sivasagar

    Every year, during the Mahashivaratri, a huge mela (fair) is organized in the Shiva temple and pilgrims visit from all parts of India to offer puja. During the Hindu month of Shravana (August September), Hare Krishna kirtan, chanting of the Hare Krishna mantra, is conducted all through the night, a major attraction to the devotees. The Dol Yatra and Rath Yatra are two annual festivals celebrated at Vishnu Dol. Durga Puja is celebrated every year with great fervour during September–October at Devidol.

How to reach there?

Situated 360km of Guwahati on the northeastern side, the city is encompassed by the Dehing rainforest at the point where the Lohit and Dihing Rivers meet. Going way back to the time of Ahom Dynasty the city’s culturally brimming with tourist spots with ancient connection and architectural masterpieces that include citadels and temples, water tanks, artificial lakes and other historical monuments. The town of Sivasagar is well connected to most major parts of Assam. The closest airport to Sivasagar is Jorhat airport. It is also connected well by road and rail. We can avail auto-rickshaws and other modes of transport in Sivasagar to reach Shiva Dol.

Best time to visit

Winter is the best time to visit this place. Monsoons receive heavy rainfall and should be avoided. Summers are hot and humid, and thus should be avoided. Maha Shivratri is one of the most important festivals that is celebrated here and is ideal time to visit to see the rich culture of the place.
